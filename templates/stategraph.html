The Chloropleth map presented a challenge, as it required state postal abbreviations where our data contained state's names only. An additional table in our SQL database was constructed to connect the names and abbreviations, allowing us to append our query which already included State group-by, Profit sum, an inner join, converting to a dictionary, and finally orienting as a list.

The visualization shows the wide range of profit and loss when sorted geographically. Hovering over the most profitable states of California and New York shows their totals more than double the next closest states. Ten states returned losses over this five year period, Texas by the widest margin.
